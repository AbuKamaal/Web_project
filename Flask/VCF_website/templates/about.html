POPgen

POPgen explores the annotation and description of single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) within five human populations. SNPs are the key controllers of genetic diversity. POPgen provides a user-friendly application that takes inputs of genomics coordinates, SNP name or gene name and retrieves SNP information such as rs id, genomic position, genotype frequency and allele frequency. We wrangled the sequencing data specifically to the human chromosome 22 from five different populations around the world.

IGSR

The International Genome Sample Resource collects data from multiple human populations through Variant Call Format (VCF) files, containing genotype/haplotype information for sequenced samples at each called SNP. IGSR provided our team with the usability and accessibility of data from the 1000 Genomes Project.

POPULATION GENOMICS


Population genomics is the study of genetic variation within and between populations and the evolutionary factors that explain this variation. Through the analysis of genetic variation, inferences from population genomic data can be applied to conservation genetics, evolutionary biology and precision medicine, etc. The advent of next-generation sequencing (NGS) technologies has provided a surge of genomic data from several populations, helping to improve our understanding of the evolutionary history in and between populations.
